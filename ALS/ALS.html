What is ALS?

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS), also known as motor neurone disease (MND) or Lou Gehrig's disease,
is a neurodegenerative neuromuscular disease that results in the progressive loss of motor neurons that control voluntary muscles.
ALS is the most common type of motor neuron disease.
