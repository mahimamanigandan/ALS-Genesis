Therapy

Most of the treatments for ALS involve managing the symptoms of the disease as it worsens. Some of them include:

Breathing Care
Mechanical ventilation is given to assist breathing, a tube is inserted in a surgically created hole at the front of the neck leading to the windpipe that connects to a respirator.

Physical Therapy
Practicing low-impact exercises regularly can help maintain cardiovascular fitness, muscle strength, and range of motion for a longer period of time.

Occupational Therapy
Adaptive equipment is recommended by an occupational therapist to perform activities such as dressing, grooming, eating and bathing.

Speech therapy
Alphabet board or pen, paper, and text-to-speech applications or computer-based equipment with the synthesized speech are used to assist communication.

Nutritional Support
Foods that are easier to swallow are prescribed to meet nutritional needs and eventually, a feeding tube will be recommended.

Psychological and social support
Psychologists and social workers provide emotional support and help with financial issues, insurance, and getting equipment, and paying for devices required.
