About

What is the reason?

    ALS affects the motor neurons which control voluntary muscle movements such as walking and talking and causes those neurons to gradually deteriorate, and eventually cause mortality. Motor neurons extend from the brain to the spinal cord to muscles throughout the body. When these neurons are damaged, they stop sending signals to the muscles, thereby reducing their function. ALS is a genetic disease which is inherited in 5% to 10% of people.
